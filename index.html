Bahia, Brazil

Bahia is one of the most culturally diverse and historic states in Brazil. It is known for its tropical beaches, rich Afro-Brazilian heritage, traditional music, and flavorful cuisine, being famous worldwide for dishes like acarajé, moqueca, vatapá, all caring strong African influences. Salvador, the capital, was the first capital of Brazil and is home to Pelourinho, a UNESCO World Heritage Site full of colorful colonial buildings. Bahia played a major role in Brazilian history and was one of the first regions colonized by the Portuguese. The state is also famous for its warm climate, colorful festivals, and welcoming people.
